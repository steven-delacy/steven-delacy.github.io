Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity benefits people in a number of ways, overcoming fears, recovering from strokes, tending to autism and ADHD to be more manageable in day-to-day applications. It could be as simple as journeling everyday for 30 minutes, sitting outside in peace or excersize. For me neuroplasticity can and hopefully will benefit with my self critical thinking. I have had the harsh realisation that I have a fixed mindset and when things become difficult I become negative, frustrated with myself and look for excuses. By staying consistent I hope to minimize this, if not just to make myself more confident within learning environments.

I have a few small things that can help me, I know I have a mixed mindset, it just determines what I am doing and my environment. Obviously if we could answer all of our problems we would have no need to connect with others and learn from them.

Here are a few ways I found I personally benefit and increase my neuroplasticity

    Playing guitar Going for walks Eating well, nourishing myself Proper sleep Self deprecative behaviour

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset, is the ability to develop ones talents and understandings through hard work, strategies and the right interactions. The things we can and can’t do aren’t hardcoded, in fact are achieved through a mixture of appropriate steps that are in itself not set in stone. Everyones way of learning is different.

I had a rather harsh realisation about myself when I found I wasn’t putting enough effort into EDA when I became frustrated because I didn’t understand something (it was The Dom!). I started making excuses and putting myself down, ultimately thinking about my education growing up.Triggering feelings and thoughts that were buried for a while.

This may sound like a cop out answer, but it’ll be closely related with the list regarding how to increase neuroplasticity (for me personally).I don’t think it’d do anyone justice looking at a multitude of things and seeing so much at the time would look like imperfections, this will likely be overwhelming.

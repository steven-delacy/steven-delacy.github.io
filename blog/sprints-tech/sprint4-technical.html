Problem solving.

Approaching a problem especially when fresh and new to a coding language is tricky. It isn’t as simple as googling what you think you should search. Code words, various articles/forums. It can easily become overwhelming when the results look nothing like what you expected.

Getting stuck on a problem happens more than often, and second guessing can be distracting. I overthought a process with how to add the first child in the Manipulating JS Objects exercise.A lot of cases simple is easier, not just for a students learning, but readability. I needed to simplify what I needed, and underestimated dot notations .. which ultimately helped me pass that point in the exercise.

Everyones approach is different

I feel more comfortable googling solutions, or even a hint at what I need to know to get the problem solving flowing. Not only is googling a great way to access information, a lot of the time you have to be oddly specific with what words you use. This helps me and my learning process understand what’s what in a plethora of material that makes up code.
